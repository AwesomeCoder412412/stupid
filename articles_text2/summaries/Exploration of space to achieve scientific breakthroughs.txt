The article discusses the challenges and potential of beta-cell replacement therapy for type 1 diabetes (T1D), highlighting recent advancements in stem cell biology and tissue engineering that may transform treatment. While these advancements demonstrate promising results, the author warns that practical implementation remains daunting due to issues such as scalability, cost, and accessibility. To address these challenges, a "Quality by Design" (QbD) approach is advocated, emphasizing the importance of designing therapies that prioritize not only scientific efficacy but also practical application and economic feasibility for widespread use.
