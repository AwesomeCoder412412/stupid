The article reviews advancements in spatial omics technologies that facilitate detailed analysis of cellular organizations and interactions at single-cell and subcellular levels. These methods enable the identification of specific cellular compartments and their molecular abundances, providing insights into cellular phenotypes and interactions, with various techniques displaying different capabilities in resolution, multiplexing, and throughput. The review highlights the evolving landscape of spatial omics, including a range of methodologies and their applications for both research and clinical uses, ultimately underscoring the significant potential for new discoveries in cellular biology and disease contexts.
