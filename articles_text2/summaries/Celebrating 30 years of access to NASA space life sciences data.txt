NASA is celebrating the 30th anniversary of the Ames Life Sciences Data Archive (ALSDA) and the 10th anniversary of GeneLab, key resources that enhance our understanding of life in space through the collection and sharing of valuable scientific data. The integration of ALSDA and GeneLab into the NASA Open Science Data Repository (OSDR) represents a significant advancement in data accessibility and research collaboration, allowing scientists to better analyze and utilize extensive datasets from various space missions. With ongoing initiatives to promote open science, these repositories facilitate interdisciplinary research and the application of modern technologies like AI to support future space exploration and health monitoring.
