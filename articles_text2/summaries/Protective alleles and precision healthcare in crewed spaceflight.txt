The article explores the potential of protective alleles in human genetics to inform precision healthcare for astronauts in crewed spaceflight. It reviews various alleles that may reduce cancer risk and improve musculoskeletal and cardiovascular health, suggesting that these genetic insights could enhance nutritional and exercise recommendations during deep space missions. The paper also discusses the challenges of translating these genetic findings into practical applications, highlighting both ethical concerns and the need for comprehensive research in the evolving field of aerospace medicine.
