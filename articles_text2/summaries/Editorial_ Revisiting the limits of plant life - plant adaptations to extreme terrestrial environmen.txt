The editorial discusses the adaptations of plants to extreme terrestrial environments and their implications for astrobiology and space biology. It emphasizes the importance of understanding plant survival strategies in harsh conditions as humanity prepares for deep space exploration, highlighting studies on plant resilience to radiation and other stressors that could inform future space missions. The article also suggests that terrestrial plants may play a critical role in sustaining life during long-duration interplanetary travel and the search for extraterrestrial life.
