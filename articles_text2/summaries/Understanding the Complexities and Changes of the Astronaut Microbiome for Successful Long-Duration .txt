The article discusses the complex relationship between the microbiome of astronauts and the unique challenges posed by long-duration space missions, such as microgravity and radiation. It highlights how these conditions can lead to alterations in the composition and function of the microbiome, which are essential for maintaining astronaut health and mitigating health risks during space travel. The review emphasizes the need for further research and integration of microbiome monitoring in the planning of future missions, particularly in light of the varied effects on microbiome dynamics caused by stressors encountered in space.
