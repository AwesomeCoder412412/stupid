The article discusses the need for advanced research on the effects of space on the human body, particularly in the context of future multi-year crewed missions to Mars and beyond. It proposes the creation of a Human Cell Space Atlas to provide a comprehensive resource for understanding human physiology in space and enhancing precision spaceflight healthcare through high-resolution omics data. Additionally, the text examines the ethical, cultural, and legal challenges associated with human space omics and emphasizes the importance of international collaboration to navigate these complexities as humanity continues to explore space.
