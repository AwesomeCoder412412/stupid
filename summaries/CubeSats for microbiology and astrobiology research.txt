The article discusses the importance of future space experiment platforms for advancing research in astrobiology and astrochemistry. It emphasizes the unique opportunities these platforms, including the International Space Station, CubeSats, and the Lunar Orbital Gateway, present for conducting groundbreaking experiments aimed at understanding the origins of life, habitability, and biosignatures. Recommendations for these experiments include utilizing in situ measurements and developing sophisticated technologies to analyze the effects of the space environment on biological and chemical processes, which could enhance our quest to discover life beyond Earth.
